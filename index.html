"The signal we cared about wasn't pageviews. It was when 'Empathy' and 'Collaboration' started showing up unprompted in performance review conversations and team meetings. §9

Potential Follow-Up Traps

Wioleta is a coach. She probes. Here are the follow-ups she's most likely to throw.

"Tell me more about that resistance — what specifically did the SME say?"

Don't generalize. Have one specific dialogue moment ready per major story.

Sample (GenAI story): "One senior medical writer told me, 'You're asking me to teach the machine to do what I do. Why would I help replace myself?' That was the moment I realized the language was wrong."

"What would you have done differently?"

Always have a real answer.

Sample (BSVwithU): "I should have insisted on the change-request framework in week one, not month three. I lost three months of trust I had to rebuild."

"That sounds like Prosci. Did you use ADKAR?"

If she names a framework, acknowledge crisply.

Sample: "Some of the moves I made map to ADKAR, yes — particularly the 'Awareness' and 'Desire' stages. But I designed them from the listening sessions, not from the framework. I find when I lead with the framework, the design gets generic."

"How would you have measured success?"

Have adoption metrics ready, not output metrics.

Sample (Change Makers Council): "Pageviews on the Credo page were vanity. The metric I cared about was whether 'Empathy' showed up unprompted in performance review conversations. We measured that through quarterly HR sampling."

"What did your reportee struggle with most?"

Be specific.

Sample (Vrinda): "Confidence in client-facing demos. Her technical understanding outpaced her presentation comfort. We did dry-runs every Tuesday for two months. I'd push back hard on weak points so the real demos felt easier."

"Why are you leaving Indegene?"

Don't disparage.

Sample: "Indegene has been formative — three promotions in five years, deep pharma exposure, the chance to build durable capability. What I'm looking for now is depth over breadth. Running one pharma's full transformation agenda inside a CxO function is a different craft than running multiple pharma portfolios from outside. Astellas at this moment — CSP2026, three GCCs, the Change Communication mandate — is the right specific opportunity, not just the right next step."
